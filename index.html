About me

I am a final-year PhD Student in Astronomy at the University of Padova, Italy, under the supervision of Prof. Raffaele Gratton. My current research deals with the discovery and characterization of exoplanets and brown dwarfs around young stars that are much more massive than the Sun. Enabled by the capabilities of SPHERE@VLT, the BEAST survey is the first direct-imaging campaign specifically looking for wide giant exoplanets around this almost unexplored class of stars. My main contribution to the survey lies in the age and mass estimate of both targets and companions, a key step to correctly interpret the detections: to this end, I've explored indirect techniques for age determination, and developed MADYS, a tool for isochronal age determination of young stars. Connecting observations and theory, my work is crucial to investigate the demographics of directly-imaged exoplanets and to understand the processes that created them.
